## Overview

The Flight Operations module manages all flight activities in SIGSAHO, from general transfers to weight & balance calculations. It provides complete operational logging, crew coordination, and regulatory compliance for all flight types.

<Note>
  Flight operations automatically integrate with aircraft management, updating flight hours, maintenance schedules, and crew duty time tracking.
</Note>

## User Workflows

### Creating a General Transfer

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Transfer Registration">
    Navigate to **Registro de Servicios > Traslados Generales** from the main menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Initiate New Transfer">
    Click **"Nuevo Traslado"** to open the transfer creation form.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Transfer Type">
    Choose the transfer classification:
    
    **Transfer Types:**
    - General (personnel/cargo transport)
    - Support (government assistance)
    - Training (flight instruction)
    - Positioning (ferry flight)
    
    **Description Type:**
    - Official mission
    - Training exercise
    - Humanitarian aid
    - Emergency response
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Flight Details">
    Complete operational information:
    
    **Date and Time**
    - Flight date and time
    - Flight duration (minutes)
    
    **Locations**
    - Origin locality/airport
    - Destination locality/airport
    - Route description
    - Travel time on route
    
    **Aircraft and Crew**
    - Aircraft assignment (from active fleet)
    - Crew member selection (pilots, flight crew)
    
    **Operational Data**
    - Requesting authority ("Solicitado por")
    - Logbook folio number
    - Fuel consumption (liters/gallons)
    - Additional passengers (if any)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Description and Notes">
    Enter mission description:
    - Purpose of flight
    - Cargo/passenger details
    - Special instructions
    - Operational notes
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Assign Crew Members">
    Select crew for the mission:
    - Pilot in command
    - First officer (if applicable)
    - Flight crew/attendants
    - Observers/passengers
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save and Complete">
    Choose completion status:
    - **In Process**: Flight planned but not completed
    - **Finished**: Flight completed, log immediately
    - **Canceled**: Flight canceled before execution
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Weight & Balance Operations

Weight and Balance (W&B) calculations are critical for flight safety:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access W&B Management">
    Navigate to **Control Aéreo > Peso y Balance** to view all W&B records.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create New W&B Sheet">
    Click **"Nueva Hoja de Carga"** and select the aircraft from the dropdown menu. Each aircraft requires its own W&B calculation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Pre-Flight Information Auto-Fills">
    The system automatically populates:
    
    **Aircraft Empty Weight Data** (from aircraft specifications):
    - Operating empty weight
    - Empty CG (center of gravity)
    - Empty moment
    
    **Flight Information**:
    - Date of calculation
    - Pilot in command selection
    - First officer selection (optional)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Load Details">
    Enter all load items in the detail rows:
    
    **Fuel**:
    - Fuel weight (gallons × fuel density)
    - Fuel arm (distance from datum)
    - Calculates fuel moment automatically
    
    **Crew**:
    - Pilot weight and station
    - Copilot weight and station
    - Crew member weights and stations
    
    **Passengers**:
    - Each passenger weight
    - Assigned station/seat position
    - Station arm from aircraft specifications
    
    **Cargo/Baggage**:
    - Cargo weight
    - Cargo compartment location
    - Compartment arm
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Calculated Totals">
    System automatically computes:
    
    **Operating with Fuel (Subtotals)**:
    - Total weight with fuel
    - CG with fuel loaded
    - MAC percentage (Mean Aerodynamic Chord)
    
    **Takeoff Totals**:
    - Total takeoff weight
    - Takeoff CG
    - Takeoff moment
    - Takeoff MAC percentage
    
    **Landing Totals**:
    - Landing weight (after fuel burn)
    - Landing CG
    - Landing moment
    - Landing MAC percentage
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Validate Weight & Balance">
    System performs automatic validation:
    
    ✓ **Weight Checks**:
    - Takeoff weight ≤ Maximum takeoff weight
    - Landing weight ≤ Maximum landing weight
    - No single station exceeds limit
    
    ✓ **CG Checks**:
    - Takeoff CG within aircraft envelope
    - Landing CG within aircraft envelope
    - CG shift acceptable throughout flight
    
    **Visual Indicators**:
    - Green checkmark: All limits satisfied
    - Red warning: Limit exceeded, unsafe for flight
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save and Print">
    Once validated:
    - Save W&B record to database
    - Generate printable W&B sheet (PDF)
    - Link to associated flight transfer
    - File with flight documentation
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key Features

### Automatic Aircraft Hour Logging

<Card title="Seamless Flight Hour Tracking" icon="clock">
  **Integration with Aircraft Management**
  
  When a transfer is marked **"Finished"**:
  
  1. System extracts flight time (in minutes)
  2. Converts to decimal hours
  3. Adds to aircraft's `current_time_flying`
  4. Recalculates `available_time` (hours until maintenance)
  5. Updates `maintenance_due_percentage`
  6. Triggers maintenance alerts if thresholds exceeded
  
  **Example:**
  - Transfer duration: 75 minutes
  - Converted to: 1.25 hours
  - Aircraft current hours: 245.50 hrs → 246.75 hrs
  - Available hours: 53.25 hrs → 52.00 hrs
  - Maintenance due: 82.2% → 83.0%
  
  All updates are **automatic and real-time** - no manual calculations needed.
</Card>

## Weight & Balance Calculations

### Understanding W&B Components

<Accordion title="Weight Terminology">
  **Operating Empty Weight (OEW)**
  - Aircraft structure
  - Engines and fixed equipment
  - Unusable fuel
  - Hydraulic fluids and oil
  - Emergency equipment
  
  **Takeoff Weight**
  - OEW + Fuel + Crew + Passengers + Cargo
  
  **Landing Weight**
  - Takeoff Weight - Fuel Burned
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Center of Gravity (CG)">
  **What is CG?**
  - The point where aircraft balances
  - Measured in inches from reference datum
  - Changes as fuel burns and weight shifts
  
  **Why CG Matters:**
  - Forward CG: Heavy elevator forces, reduced range
  - Aft CG: Unstable, potential loss of control
  - Must remain within envelope throughout flight
  
  **CG Calculation:**
  ```
  CG = Total Moment / Total Weight
  ```
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Moment">
  **Definition:**
  - Weight × Arm (distance from datum)
  - Measured in pound-inches (lb-in)
  
  **Purpose:**
  - Represents rotational force around datum
  - Used to calculate CG position
  
  **Example:**
  - Passenger weight: 180 lbs
  - Seat arm: 120 inches
  - Moment: 180 × 120 = 21,600 lb-in
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Mean Aerodynamic Chord (MAC)">
  **What is MAC?**
  - Reference chord of the wing
  - Represents average wing width
  - Used to express CG as percentage
  
  **MAC Percentage:**
  - CG expressed as % MAC
  - Easier to compare across aircraft types
  - Standard format for CG limits
  
  **Example:**
  - CG at 25% MAC: CG is at 25% of wing chord
  - Typical limits: 15% - 35% MAC
</Accordion>

## Best Practices

<Accordion title="Accurate Flight Time Logging">
  - Record **actual** flight time, not scheduled time
  - Use Hobbs meter or tach time from aircraft
  - Include only block-to-block time (engine start to shutdown)
  - Do not include ground taxi time separately
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Weight & Balance Safety">
  - **Always** calculate W&B before flight
  - Verify passenger weights (use actual, not estimates)
  - Account for all cargo and baggage
  - Recalculate if passenger/cargo changes
  - Do not exceed any weight or CG limit - ever
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Crew Assignment Accuracy">
  - Assign actual crew who flew the mission
  - Verify pilot certifications are current
  - Check duty time limits before scheduling
  - Log crew changes if substitutions occur
</Accordion>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="W&B Shows Weight Limit Exceeded">
  **Cause:** Total weight exceeds maximum takeoff or landing weight
  
  **Solution:**
  1. Reduce fuel load (calculate minimum required + reserve)
  2. Reduce passenger count
  3. Reduce cargo weight
  4. Use a larger aircraft if available
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="W&B Shows CG Out of Limits">
  **Cause:** Center of gravity outside safe envelope
  
  **Solution:**
  1. Redistribute passengers to different seats
  2. Move cargo to different compartments
  3. Adjust fuel load (check if fuel affects CG)
  4. Recalculate with actual passenger weights
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Cannot Mark Transfer as Finished">
  **Cause:** Missing required fields or invalid data
  
  **Solution:**
  1. Verify aircraft is assigned
  2. Ensure at least one crew member assigned
  3. Check flight duration is > 0
  4. Verify origin and destination are selected
</Accordion>
